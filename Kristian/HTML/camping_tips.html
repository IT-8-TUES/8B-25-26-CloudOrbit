Camping Tips for Beginners

Simple, practical advice to help you have a safe, comfortable, and memorable camping experience every time.

Check the Weather Before You Leave

Always look up the forecast for your destination a few days in advance. Pack accordingly — rain gear if needed, and extra layers for cold nights. Weather in mountains or forests can change quickly.

Choose a Safe and Legal Campsite

Only camp in designated or permitted areas. Wild camping may be restricted in many places. Research your site beforehand, read reviews, and check whether you need a booking or permit.

Bring More Water Than You Think You Need

Dehydration is a real risk outdoors. Carry at least 2–3 litres of water per person per day. If you're near a natural source, bring a water filter or purification tablets as a backup.

Practice Setting Up Your Tent Before the Trip

Don't try to figure out your tent for the first time in the dark or rain. Practise at home first so you know exactly how it goes up, and check for any missing parts or tears.

Arrive Before Dark

Setting up camp in daylight is much easier and safer. Aim to arrive at your campsite with at least 2 hours of daylight to spare so you can settle in comfortably.

Keep Food Away from Wildlife

Store food in sealed containers or a bear-proof bag, away from your sleeping area. Never leave food out overnight — it attracts animals and can become dangerous.

Wear Layers of Clothing

Temperatures can drop significantly at night even in summer. Wear moisture-wicking base layers, an insulating mid-layer, and a waterproof outer shell. You can always take layers off if it's warm.

Keep Your Campsite Clean

Pick up all your rubbish and leave the campsite exactly as you found it — or better. Respect nature and other campers. Follow the 'Leave No Trace' principles at all times.

Save Your Phone Battery

Turn on low-power mode, reduce brightness, and switch off Wi-Fi when not needed. Bring a power bank for emergencies. Your phone could be a lifeline if something goes wrong.

Leave No Trace Behind

Take all your rubbish with you when you leave. Do not disturb plants, animals, or natural features. The outdoors is shared by everyone — be a respectful visitor.

Quick Bonus Tips

  • Tell someone your plans — where you're going and when you'll return.

  • Pack a physical map even if you have a phone — signal can disappear.

  • Keep a small notebook and pen in your pack for notes and sketches.

  • Wear sunscreen even on cloudy days — UV rays still get through.

  • Start small — try a one-night campsite close to home before a bigger trip.
