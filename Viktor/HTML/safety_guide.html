Stay Safe Outdoors

Camping Safety Guide


Being prepared is the key to a safe trip. Follow these guidelines to protect
yourself and your companions in the great outdoors.

In a real emergency, call your local emergency services immediately.

UK: 999 | Europe: 112 | USA/Canada: 911 | Australia: 000 — Always keep your phone charged and share your location with someone before heading out.

Fire Safety

  • Always follow the campsite's fire rules and regulations.
  • Keep a bucket of water or sand near the campfire at all times.
  • Fully extinguish the fire before going to sleep or leaving the site.
  • Use only the designated fire pit if one is provided.
  • Never light a fire during a fire ban — check local alerts before you go.
  • Never leave a fire unattended — not even for a few minutes.
  • Keep fire at least 3 metres away from your tent and vegetation.

Wildlife Safety

  • Never feed wild animals — it is dangerous for both you and them.
  • Keep a safe distance from all wildlife and never approach them.
  • If you see a bear or large predator, stay calm and back away slowly.
  • Use insect repellent to protect against ticks and mosquitoes.
  • Store all food in sealed, bear-proof containers away from your tent.
  • Dispose of food scraps and rubbish properly — do not leave anything out.
  • Shake out shoes and clothing before putting them on each morning.

Weather Safety

  • Check the detailed weather forecast for your area before departing.
  • Seek shelter in a low-lying area away from water during lightning storms.
  • Hypothermia can occur even in mild temperatures — always wear dry layers.
  • Pack a waterproof poncho even if the forecast looks sunny.
  • If a thunderstorm approaches, get away from tall trees and open ground.
  • In extreme heat, stay hydrated and rest in shade during the hottest hours.
  • Have a clear evacuation plan if conditions worsen unexpectedly.

First Aid

  • Always carry a well-stocked first aid kit on every camping trip.
  • Carry any personal medication you need and tell your companions about it.
  • For bites, stings, or allergic reactions, seek medical help immediately.
  • Keep the first aid kit accessible, not buried at the bottom of your bag.
  • Know the basics: how to clean and dress a wound, treat a burn, and deal with blisters.
  • Know the signs of heat exhaustion and hypothermia.
  • Do not attempt to remove deeply embedded foreign objects — get medical help.

Emergency Planning

  • Always tell a trusted person your exact route, campsite, and return date.
  • Keep your phone charged — use a power bank to top it up daily.
  • Know the nearest hospital, ranger station, or emergency contact for your area.
  • If lost, stay put and signal for help rather than wandering further.
  • Save the local emergency number in your phone before you leave.
  • Carry a physical map and compass in case your phone dies or loses signal.
  • Have a clear meeting point and emergency plan with your group.

The Golden Rule of Outdoor Safety

Preparation prevents most camping emergencies. Research your destination, pack the right gear, tell someone your plans, and always trust your instincts. If something feels unsafe, it probably is — prioritise your wellbeing over the adventure.
